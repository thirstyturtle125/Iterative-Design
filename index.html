Critique Feedback Received

The feedback that we received at our critique studio was crucial in the final iteration of our prototype. Some notable feedback that guided our final design choices included:

  • The interactions users had with buttons weakened learnability.
  • Returning to previous pages was difficult.
  • The "Home" and "Templates" page seemed to be switched.
  • There was no way to actually contact the company.
  • The intuitiveness of the navigation sidebar could be improved.
  • The editor page should appear more like a text editor.

User Testing Results & Analysis

In addition to the feedback our team received in the critique studio, we received feedback from three user testers. We gave them three tasks:

  1. Share the "Neural Networks" directory from the Files page.
  2. Begin editing with a Decision Tree template.
  3. Read about the website & company from the "About Us" page.

Here are links to videos of their feedback, as well as summaries of their feedback:

  • User 1 (Link to video)
    • Very easy to get through navigation to share files.
    • Some user interactions are confusing and not entirely intuitive.
    • Some wording on the prototype is ambiguous.
  • User 2 (Link to video)
    • Navigation of site was easy to figure out.
    • Template selection should be clearer on Home page.
    • Incredibly easy to find information on company.
  • User 3 (Link to video)
    • Share button should also be on individual files.
    • User became confused by Figma layout and was not able to complete last two tasks
Analysis

After reviewing the User Testing feedback, our group recognized some important insights within our results.

The results of first task indicates that the layout of the editor is pristine. This is in line with our expectations because we based this design decision on feedback we received during our crit studio.

The response to our second task indicates that we should have kept template selection on its own page, contrary to the feedback we received in the crit studio. This was unexpcted. Moving forward, we believe that keeping things modular here would be best for learnability.

Finally, the results of our last task fell in line with our expectations, as we made it incredibly easy to reach the "About" page.

Overall, we believe that just a few interface changes regarding the interactive components of our prototype should be changed to be a bit more obvious that they are interactive.
